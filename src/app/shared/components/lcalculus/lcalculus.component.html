Discontinuities

Discontinuities can be classified as:

  • Jump: both one-sided limits exist, but have different values
  • Infinite: both one-sided limits are infinite
  • Endpoint: only one of the one-sided limits exists
  • Mixed: at least one of the one-sided limits does not exist
  • Removable: are characterized by the fact that the limit exists but f(a) is different to the limit. Removable discontinuities can be "fixed" by re-defining the function.
